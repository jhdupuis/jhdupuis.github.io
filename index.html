Thoughts on Product Marketing

The purpose of product marketing is to align the company's value and capabilities with market and customer needs. It is responsible for providing a clear explanation of how and why the company and its products are valuable, and for driving expansion by demonstrating this value. Good product marketing influences every stage of the customer journey, from awareness and consideration to evaluation and onboarding.

To get there, product marketing teams need to develop:

  • Deep knowledge of customers, marketing, competitors, and the product
  • Cross-functional relationships to help them take ownership of the team's messages

Good product marketing can help a company achieve its goals by improving the effectiveness of the marketing team with a thorough understanding of the product, increasing the efficiency of the commercial teams through targeted content and right-fit sales strategies, and providing buyers and users with relevant content to advance in their journey. Finally, product marketing helps the product team respond more quickly to market, segment, analyst, and competitor news.

I love Emma Stratton's work on punchy messaging. Great messaging and positioning starts with VBF.

  • V: Value proposition – kick things off with a big goal that resonates
  • B: Benefits – here you can show off what your product provides
  • F: Features – the last part explains how the product achieves benefits

A benefit is something that an individual can achieve. For example, if your product helps an engineer save time by filtering through noisy data, that would be a benefit. A value proposition is something that a team, department, or organization as a whole achieves. For instance, if an engineering team consistently meets its OKR targets because of the product, that would be a value proposition.

  • Value proposition: Fix issues before end customers even notice.
  • Benefit: See your entire stack’s performance in one place.
  • Feature: A dashboard that consolidates performance data.

From this, the message would be:

Resolve issues before customers notice them. Stop problems before they start by monitoring your entire stack’s performance in one dashboard.
